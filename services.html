Common Questions

Frequently Asked

Answers to the questions we hear most from clients across Kenya.

How long does it take to train a detection dog?
Basic detection imprinting takes 6–8 weeks. Full operational certification to NASDU standards typically takes 3–4 months. Dogs then undergo annual recertification to maintain their operational status.
Do you supply guard dogs for homes in Nairobi?
Yes. We supply trained guard dogs for residential properties across Nairobi and Kenya. Each dog comes with a handler orientation session so your family or guards know how to work with the animal safely and effectively.
Can you train my existing dog?
Yes, subject to a temperament and drive assessment. Not all dogs are suitable for security or detection work. We assess your dog first and provide an honest evaluation. If suitable, we design a custom program. If not, we advise on the best path forward.
What areas in Kenya do you serve?
We are based in Kitengela and serve all of Nairobi and Kenya's major counties. We have deployed to Mombasa, Kisumu, Eldoret, Nakuru and beyond. We also operate in Uganda and Tanzania. Contact us to discuss your location.
How much does it cost to buy a trained guard dog?
Pricing depends on breed, level of training and certification. Basic obedience dogs start lower; fully certified NASDU patrol or detection dogs are higher due to the training investment. WhatsApp us at +254 721 448 555 for a specific quote.
Is your handler training open to individuals or only companies?
Both. We run handler training for individuals who own dogs, for security companies building K9 units, and for government agencies. Individual and group rates are available. NASDU certification pathways are open to all qualifying candidates.
